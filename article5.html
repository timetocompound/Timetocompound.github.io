In the investment world, ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds) have revolutionized how we build wealth. Instead of looking for a needle in a haystack, you can just buy the whole haystack.

What is an ETF?
Imagine a basket containing stocks from hundreds of companies. By buying one share of the ETF, you automatically own a small piece of every company inside it.

The Crucial Choice: Acc vs Dist

When searching for an ETF (e.g., S&P 500), you will see two versions of the same product. The difference lies in how they handle dividends.

Dividends are paid out to your account as cash (usually quarterly or semi-annually).

Reinvestment

Dividends are NOT paid to you. The ETF automatically uses them to buy more shares.

  • âœ… Automatic Compounding
  • âœ… Tax Efficiency
  • âŒ No cash in hand

Why Accumulating Wins Long-Term

The magic lies in compound interest. When dividends are reinvested, they buy new shares, which in turn generate new dividends. It's a snowball effect.
