If the stock market were a supermarket, buying individual stocks would be like trying to pick the best eggs one by one. An ETF (Exchange Traded Fund) is like buying the whole basket.

What is an ETF?

An ETF is a "basket" containing hundreds or thousands of stocks. By buying **one** share of the ETF, you automatically own a slice of all the companies inside it.

The most famous example is an ETF tracking the **S&P 500**. With one move, you invest in the 500 largest companies in America (Apple, Microsoft, Google, etc.).

The Big Decision: Accumulating vs. Distributing

When choosing an ETF, you will see the tags (Acc) or (Dist) next to the name. This is crucial for your taxes and returns.
