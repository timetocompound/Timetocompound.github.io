The crucial two-day Federal Reserve (FED) meeting began today, with the interest rate decision expected tomorrow (Wednesday). Global markets are on standby, as Jerome Powell's move will determine the direction of the S&P 500, the Dollar, and Bitcoin for the coming months.

ğŸ“ˆ The Central Scenario (The Pause)

The broad market consensus leans towards maintaining interest rates at current levels (Pause). The FED has repeatedly stated it will remain "Data Dependent," basing its decisions on the latest economic figures.

Due to the recent slowdown in inflation, pressure for a new rate hike has significantly decreased. However, the risk of a resurgence of inflationary pressures remains.

Key Decision-Making Factors:

Data Point Current Status
Inflation (CPI) Lower than peak, but above the 2% target.
Labor Market Remains robust (leaving little room for immediate relaxation).
FED Goal Keeping rates high for "a long time" (Higher for longer).

ğŸ’£ The 3 Market Scenarios (What investors should watch)

The reaction of the S&P 500 and cryptocurrency will depend not only on the decision but primarily on **Jerome Powell's forward guidance**.

  1. **Scenario 1: Pause & "Hawkish" Rhetoric (Bearish)**
    The FED pauses rates but Powell states it's too early to discuss cuts in 2026.
    **Market Reaction:** Slight dip or stabilization. The market will be disappointed as it anticipates signs of future cuts.
  2. **Scenario 2: Pause & "Dovish" Rhetoric (Bullish)**
    The FED pauses and Powell acknowledges inflation progress, leaving the door open for a potential cut in mid-2026.
    **Market Reaction:** Rally in tech stocks and Bitcoin. The dollar weakens.
  3. **Scenario 3: Unexpected Hike (High Risk/Crash)**
    If the FED unexpectedly raises rates by 25 basis points, citing strong growth.
    **Market Reaction:** Sharp drop in the S&P 500 and major disruption, as the market hasn't priced this in. (Very low probability).
Key Takeaway: The focus will be on the "Dot Plot" and how aggressive (Hawkish) or friendly (Dovish) Powell sounds about future rate cuts. The Pause is largely priced in.
