The term "Blockchain" sounds complex, but its core idea is simple. Imagine a huge digital ledger that is open to everyone, but no one can erase or change what has been written.

The "Glass Box" Analogy

Think of the blockchain as a chain of transparent, glass boxes:

  1. The Box (Block): Data is put inside (e.g., "John sent 1 Bitcoin to Mary").
  2. The Lock (Hash): Once the box is full, it is locked with a unique digital code.
  3. The Chain: The next box contains the code of the previous one. If someone tries to tamper with the old box, the chain breaks, and everyone sees it.

Why is it so Secure? (Decentralization)

Security doesn't come from a single code but from decentralization. Instead of one bank holding the ledger (Central Authority), millions of computers (Nodes) worldwide hold copies simultaneously.

If a hacker wanted to steal Bitcoin, they would need to compromise 51% of all network computers globally within 10 minutes. This is virtually impossible with current technology.

Beyond Cryptocurrencies

Blockchain is not just for Bitcoin. It is already being used for:

  • Smart Contracts: Agreements that execute automatically without lawyers.
  • Supply Chain Management: Tracking products from the factory to the shelf.
  • Digital Identity: Secure storage of personal data.
